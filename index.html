Inefficiency in the Reductionist Approach to Solving the Human Genome

Why has reductionism only gone so far in solving the human genome? This article examines the limitations of a bottom-up approach when dealing with the vast complexity of genomic systems, from DNA sequences to protein interactions and beyond.

The human genome is made up of hundreds of thousands of elements, with approximately 63,000 potentially functional genes, 252,000 gene products, and complex interactions across multiple biological levels.
