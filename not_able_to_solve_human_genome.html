DNA molecules are Turning tapes

Why has reductionism only done so far in solving the human genome?

The bottom-up approach of dissecting individual constituents parts of the genome to understand a physiological process or even just a biological process like the cell cycle has only taken so far as to label all the elements (RNA, protein molecules) that are involved in the process and some of the mechanisms involved in their interactions with other elements. However, it has not been possible, in the case of the cell cycle (one of the many processes involved in cancer formation), to control the process to a desired need/target goal.

This can be explained by considering all the elements involved in a given process. For example, in the cell cycle, the gene regulatory network involved in it not only includes the role of coding but also non-coding genes. There is interaction to control the process at three different layers, from DNA-DNA interactions to RNA-DNA interaction in the nucleus, from protein-protein interactions at cytoplasm to intercellular interactions that are involved in most of the given processes. In other words, it has not been possible to make the system behave to reach desirable states.
